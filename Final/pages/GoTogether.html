Overview

The Problem

Planning group trips is stressful and inefficient. Travelers struggle to coordinate itineraries, split expenses, and communicate with their group, often relying on multiple apps that don't integrate with each other.

The Solution

GoTogether is a mobile application that simplifies group travel planning by providing collaborative tools for itinerary creation, expense splitting, and real-time coordinationâ€”all in one place.

Project Goals

  • Reduce the stress of planning group trips
  • Minimize miscommunication between travelers
  • Create a centralized platform for all travel planning needs
  • Make expense tracking and splitting effortless

User Research

Understanding the Problem

I conducted interviews with 12 frequent group travelers to understand their pain points when planning trips with others. Here's what I learned:

  • 100% of participants used 3+ apps to plan their last group trip
  • 83% reported frustration with splitting expenses
  • 75% had conflicts due to miscommunication about plans
  • 67% found itinerary coordination to be the most stressful part

Competitive Analysis

I analyzed existing travel apps to identify gaps in the market:

TripIt

Good for individual itineraries but lacks group collaboration features

Splitwise

Excellent for expense splitting but no travel-specific features

Google Trips

Discontinued service that didn't focus on group travel

User Personas

The Planner

"I always end up organizing everything. I wish my friends could help more without me having to chase them."

  • Wants collaborative tools
  • Needs to delegate tasks
  • Frustrated with last-minute changes

The Free Spirit

"I just want to show up and have fun. All this planning stresses me out."

  • Prefers minimal planning
  • Wants easy access to info
  • Forgets to pay people back

Design & Iteration

Initial Sketches

I began by sketching low-fidelity wireframes to explore different layouts and user flows.

Key Features

Shared Itinerary

Collaboratively build your trip schedule with all group members

Expense Splitting

Track shared expenses and automatically calculate who owes what

Group Chat

Communicate with your travel group without switching apps

Location Sharing

See where your group members are when exploring separately

Usability Testing

I conducted usability tests with 5 participants to identify pain points in the prototype:

  • Users wanted clearer visual hierarchy in the itinerary view
  • The expense splitting flow needed simplification
  • Some icons weren't immediately intuitive

Final Solution

Onboarding

A simple onboarding process helps users quickly create or join a trip.

Trip Dashboard

The central hub shows all trip information at a glance with easy navigation to key features.

Itinerary

The collaborative itinerary allows group members to add and edit activities with real-time updates.

Expenses

Users can easily add expenses and the app automatically calculates balances.

Reflection

What I Learned

This project taught me the importance of thorough user research in identifying real pain points. What initially seemed like a simple coordination problem actually involved deeper issues around social dynamics and financial transparency in group settings.

I also learned to value early and frequent usability testing. Some of my initial assumptions about user behavior were proven wrong during testing, leading to valuable iterations.

Next Steps

  • Conduct additional testing with larger groups (5+ people)
  • Explore integration with popular booking services
  • Develop a web version for easier pre-trip planning
  • Add more customization options for itinerary views
