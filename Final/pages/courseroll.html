CourseRoll

A platform for creating and sharing interactive course materials

Role

UX/UI Designer

Timeline

4 months

Tools

Figma, Adobe XD

Overview

The Problem

Educators struggle to create engaging, interactive course materials that work across different devices and platforms. Students often find traditional digital course materials static and uninspiring, leading to decreased engagement.

The Solution

CourseRoll is a web-based platform that enables educators to easily create interactive course materials with embedded quizzes, videos, and collaborative elements, while providing students with a seamless learning experience across devices.

Project Goals

  • Simplify the creation of interactive course content
  • Increase student engagement with course materials
  • Support multiple content types (text, video, quizzes)
  • Ensure accessibility across devices

User Research

Understanding the Problem

I conducted interviews with 15 educators and surveyed 50 students to understand pain points in digital course material creation and consumption:

  • 87% of educators spent excessive time formatting materials for different platforms
  • 73% of students reported difficulty staying engaged with static PDFs or slides
  • 65% of educators wanted to include interactive elements but lacked technical skills
  • 92% of students preferred materials with embedded questions and progress tracking

Competitive Analysis

Analysis of existing educational platforms revealed key opportunities:

Google Classroom

Excellent for assignment distribution but limited content creation tools

Moodle

Powerful but complex interface discourages many educators

Nearpod

Great interactivity but focused on live sessions rather than asynchronous learning

User Personas

The Busy Professor

"I want to create engaging materials without spending hours learning new software."

  • Needs intuitive, time-saving tools
  • Wants to repurpose existing content
  • Values student engagement metrics

The Digital Native Student

"I learn better when I can interact with the material, not just read it."

  • Expects mobile-friendly content
  • Prefers bite-sized, interactive elements
  • Wants progress tracking

Usability Testing

Testing with 8 educators and 12 students revealed important insights:

  • Educators needed clearer visual cues for the content creation process
  • Students wanted more prominent progress indicators
  • Both groups requested better navigation between course sections

Final Solution

Content Creation

The intuitive editor allows educators to build courses by dragging and dropping elements, with real-time previews.

Course Dashboard

Educators can organize materials into modules and track student progress at a glance.

Student View

Students experience content in a clean, distraction-free interface with interactive elements.

Mobile Experience

The responsive design ensures optimal viewing on phones and tablets.

Reflection

What I Learned

This project reinforced the importance of designing for two distinct user groups (educators and students) with very different needs and technical abilities. Creating a system that was powerful enough for content creators while remaining simple and intuitive for consumers was a challenging but rewarding balance to achieve.

I also gained valuable experience in designing for accessibility across devices, ensuring the platform worked equally well on desktop computers in classrooms and mobile devices used by students on the go.
